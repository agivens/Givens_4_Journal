Shareware is a type of proprietary software that is initially free of charge
to users, who are encouraged to make and share copies. Software that runs for
a limited amount of time before it expires. Software that displays or
downloads advertising material when user is online. Crippleware is software
that does not work properly until the user buys a fully functional version. 
Freemium is when a product or service is provided, free of charge, but money is
charged for other features. Freeware is software that is free, that may have 
restrictions placed on it. Richard Stallman is the founder of GNU Project and
Free Software Foundation. Eben Moglen is the founder of Software Freedom
Law Center. Lawrence Lessig is the founder of Creative Commons.
